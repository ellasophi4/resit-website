Implementing CARP principles in my work – Pick Your Own Pumpkins Task:

Outlined in Robin Williams book “The Non-Designers Design Book”, the CARP principles are as follows:
C = Contrast; it can refer to contrast in the forms of colour, font weights, type of font etc.
A = Alignment; items in design should be aligned together for both ease and aesthetics.
R = Repetition, repetition should be used to ensure a cohesive design, this could be through colour, font, layouts etc.
P = Proximity; similar items should be grouped together to show their similarity and used to draw the eye to specific information in the intended order.
In my reimagination of the PYO pumpkins poster I used contrast in a few different ways. Knowing I’d likely want to be using some orange in the poster due to its theme I quickly decided on a pale blue background which contrasted the array of orange colours I eventually chose for both my large title and pumpkin shapes. I also opted to use contrast in my font choices – my large title and some highlighted information used a Serif font to draw the eye, compared to other text on the poster which used sans-serif for readability at smaller sizes.
For alignment I initially used a rigid grid to separate my text and images, however, after playing around with the offsetting of some items and moving them into a more relaxed grid with items overlapping slightly, I preferred the look and felt it fit the event’s theme as it looked more organic.
Repetition is evident throughout my poster. I stuck to using two fonts – one serif, one sans-serif and repeated them both throughout my work. I also used the same tones of orange in both my title and images to make the poster look cohesive from a distance. The pumpkin image was also repeated multiple times in the poster which was intended to create some separation in the sections of the poster.
In trying to use the proximity concept well, I began by breaking up the copy into chunks I felt went together. I then created areas of separation to house each ‘chunk’. I introduced some movement in the text by choosing abnormal paths whilst ensuring the text remains readable. I think that my poster was successful in creating a product more visually pleasing than the source material and I am happy with the colour scheme and fonts chosen. I was happy to have an opportunity to experiment with shapes in order to enhance my design. I think opportunities to enhance my work would include reconfiguring my bulky copy in the bottom left and experimenting with different flows to more closely guide the viewer to information in a specific order.

Typography Reflections

Axial: For this poster I used the perspective grid to make an axis to hinge my information off of. I enjoy the slight distortion of the lettering and the illusion of floating. I like the layout and think that all of the text is readable.
Bilateral: I liked this layout although having all the text towards the centre line of the poster left lots of unused space in each corner. The highlighted text draws your eye first. I think the bulk copy could be made easier to read however the contrast helps.
Dilatational: Initially I struggled for ideas for this system however I am pleased with the final outcome. I like the colours I have chosen and think they read well. I opted to not have all of the posters text on a curve as it became overwhelming quickly and hard to read.
Random: This poster is slightly ugly however that is what I really enjoy about it. I like to imagine the viewer having to reorientate themselves, randomly. There is a hierarchy set out with the size of fonts and their readability against the background. Although it might not be the most effective poster from a marketing point of view, I like this poster due to the exploration.
Grid: I really like this poster – I used the same colour scheme for a few of the posters. The grid system was easy to use but left lots of options for configuration. I settled on this blocky design stretching the bigger text to fit into the colour blocks to highlight the text further.
Radial: Although I like the final look of this poster with both colour choice and overall finish I do think that in places it could be improved, maybe by using more of the yellow to differentiate where parts of the blue text bleed into one another. I think it leaves a nice amount of negative space on the poster.
Transitional: I found this system the hardest to find an idea for that I liked. The final product is a little underwhelming although I do like the way the text moves with the circle background design. I do feel this poster looks heavier on the right side.
Modular: I really like this design due to the use of shape and colour. I think visually it is striking, and I liked the use of the modular system to break up my information easily.
